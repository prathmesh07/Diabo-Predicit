Problem Statement

Clinical decisions are often made based on doctors’ experience and intuition rather than on the knowledge-rich data hidden in the data. This leads to errors and high costs that affect the quality of the medical services. The use of analytic tools and data modelling can aid in the improvement of clinical decisions. Thus, the goal here is to build a web application to help doctors diagnose heart diseases using a web browser.


Feature

We collected the heart disease dataset from UCI ML. The dataset has the following 14 attributes:

age: age in years.
sex: sex (1=male; 0=female).
cp: chest pain type (0 = typical angina; 1 = atypical angina; 2 = non-anginal pain; 3: asymptomatic).
trestbps: resting blood pressure in mm Hg on admission to the hospital.
chol: serum cholesterol in mg/dl.
restecg: resting electrocardiographic results ( 0=normal; 1=having ST-T wave abnormality; 2=probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy).
thalach: maximum heart rate achieved.
exang: exercise induced angina (1=yes; 0=no).
oldpeak: ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.
slope: the slope of the peak exercise ST segment (0=upsloping; 1=flat; 2=downsloping).
ca: number of major vessels (0–3) colored by flourosopy.
thal: thalassemia (3=normal; 6=fixed defect; 7=reversable defect).
target: heart disease (1=no, 2=yes).



Result

We are able to predict whether the person has heart disease or not.
